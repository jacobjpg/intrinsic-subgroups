Let E be an elliptic curve over a field k. Yamazaki, Yang, Yoo and Yu defined the *intrinsic subgroup* E(k)_tors^is of E as the kernel of a certain symmetric biadditive pairing on the torsion points of E. They classify the possible intrinsic subgroups of an elliptic curve over Q. Let E be a curve over Q with cyclic torsion of order N = 2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,10, or 12, and P=(x0,y0) be a generator of E(k)_tors. Using their methods, it is possible to explicitly define rational functions of the Weierstrass coefficients of E and the coordinates of P, fN(E,P) = fN(a1,a2,a3,a4,a6,x0,y0), such that E(k)\_tors^is has order divisible by M iff fN(E) = ±s^(N/M) for some rational parameter s. A similar computation is possible for curves with non-cyclic torsion.

This code implements the cyclic and non-cyclic cases for curves over Q in Sage.
