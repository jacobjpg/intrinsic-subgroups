Let $E$ be an elliptic curve over a field $k$. Yamazaki, Yang, Yoo and Yu defined the *intrinsic subgroup* $E(k)\_\mathrm{tors}^\mathrm{is}$ of $E$ as the kernel of a certain symmetric biadditive pairing on the torsion points of $E$. They classify the possible intrinsic subgroups of an elliptic curve over the rational numbers. Let $E$ be a curve over $k$ with cyclic torsion of order $N = 2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,10,12$, and $P=(x0,y0)$ be a generator of $E(k)_\mathrm{tors}$. Using their methods, it is possible to explicitly define rational functions of the Weierstrass coefficients of $E$ and the coordinates of $P$, $f_N(E,P) = f_N(a1,a2,a3,a4,a6,x0,y0)$, such that $E(k)\_\mathrm{tors}^\mathrm{is}$ has order divisible by $M$ iff $f_N(E)$ is of the form $\zeta s^{N/M}$ for $\zeta \in \mu(k)$ and $s \in k$. A similar computation is possible for the cases of curves with non-cyclic torsion allowed by Mazur's theorem (namely, torsion $C_2 \oplus C\_{2N}$ for $N=1,2,3,4$).

This code implements the cyclic and non-cyclic cases for curves over the rationals in Sage.
